Privacy
policy.

How we collect, use, disclose, and protect your personal information — including personal health information — in our custody or under our control.

This Policy applies to Isorropia Registered Massage Therapy & Wellness Centre (“Isorropia”, “we”, “us”, “our”), its employees, contractors, and subcontractors (including computer support, students, volunteers, locums, and cleaners). It governs the management of all personal information collected, used, and disclosed by Isorropia for the services we offer.

We are committed to collecting, using, and disclosing personal information responsibly. Personal information is any information about a specific, identifiable individual, including name, address, telephone number, and personal history. It includes personal health information, meaning diagnostic, treatment, and care information relating to a specific individual.

By accessing our website or using any of our services, you agree to be bound by the terms of this Privacy Policy. Your use of our website is also subject to our Terms & Conditions.

British Columbia's Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA) sets out the rules for how businesses such as ours can collect, use, and disclose personal information. As a service provider to public bodies such as ICBC and WorkSafeBC, we are also committed to compliance with the Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act (FIPPA).

Collection, use & disclosure

We may collect, use, or disclose personal information as set out below and as otherwise required or permitted by law. For example, we may collect your name, gender, date of birth, address, email address, phone number, payment information, general health concerns, health card number, physician, diagnosis, referrals, and treatment plans.

Health care services

We collect, use, and disclose your personal information for the purposes of assessment, planning, treatment, and delivering other health-related services. We collect only the personal information we need to provide you with care. In the course of providing care, your information may be shared with health information custodians involved in your care unless you instruct otherwise.

Consent

Unless the purpose for collecting personal information is obvious and you voluntarily provide it, we will communicate the purpose before or at the time of collection. You have the right to refuse to provide all or part of the information, though this may affect our ability to deliver quality care. Your information may be shared without your consent only where you are judged to be at serious imminent risk of harm, where a child or vulnerable adult is at risk, where records are subpoenaed, or as otherwise required by law.

Safeguards & personnel access

We permit personnel to access personal information only to the extent necessary to perform their designated functions. We maintain appropriate physical, technological, and organizational safeguards — including access controls, encryption, and two-factor authentication — to protect personal information against loss, theft, and unauthorized access. No method of transmission or storage is perfectly secure, but these risks are minimized by the measures we employ.

You play an important role in protecting your information. We ask that you password-protect your device, email, and Jane App account with strong, unique passwords, and choose a quiet, private location when receiving care.

Payment

We may collect, use, and disclose your personal information to facilitate or collect payment for our services, or to determine entitlement to insurance coverage or other benefits.

Cookies & analytics

Our website uses cookies to track usage statistics and remember your preferences. Cookies are small pieces of information stored by your browser. We use analytics to understand how our website is used so we can improve it; analytics information is used for internal purposes only. You can set your browser to disable or delete cookies — please consult your browser's help menu for instructions.

Marketing

We may send you occasional emails about news, offers, and events we believe you'll find valuable. If you no longer wish to receive marketing communications, you may unsubscribe through the link at the bottom of every email. This will not opt you out of appointment or booking emails, or emails regarding changes to our terms and policies.

Storage of personal information

Isorropia is the custodian of all patient records. We store your personal information in electronic format on servers located within Canada, using systems with restricted access. We also use third-party software and service providers (for example, payment processing and our booking platform); some may operate outside of Canada. Unless we notify you otherwise, we retain your personal information for a period of 16 years from the last date of service, after which it is permanently deleted and destroyed.

Access, correction & complaints

You may make a written request to see and/or obtain a copy of your health record. In limited circumstances we may restrict access (for example, where disclosure could pose a risk of serious harm). If you believe your record is inaccurate or incomplete, you may request a correction in writing, and we will make the correction where appropriate or note your request in the record.

Changes to this policy

We reserve the right to amend this Policy at any time to reflect changes in our practices and to comply with applicable privacy legislation. Please check back periodically. You may request a copy of the Policy currently in effect at any time by contacting us.
