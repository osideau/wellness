— Reception hours

Our reception team is available from 8:30 AM to 4:30 PM. If we are unable to answer your call, please leave a voicemail and we will return it as soon as possible. You may also contact your therapist directly through the Jane App.

What is Registered Massage Therapy (RMT)?

Registered Massage Therapy is a regulated healthcare profession in BC focused on assessing and treating soft-tissue and musculoskeletal conditions. RMTs use evidence-informed manual techniques to reduce pain, improve mobility, and support overall well-being.

Do I need a doctor's referral?

Most patients do not need a referral to see an RMT or an acupuncturist. However, some extended health plans may require one for reimbursement — check with your insurance provider if you're unsure.

What to expect during your initial visit.

Please arrive a few minutes early and ensure that the health history form emailed to you after booking your appointment is fully completed before you arrive. All sections must be filled out — do not leave any areas blank — as this information is required to provide safe and effective care.

1. Health Intake & Confidential History
Your practitioner will review your completed health history form and discuss any relevant details in a private, confidential setting.

2. Clinical Interview & Assessment
A thorough clinical interview and physical assessment helps identify the nature of your condition, contributing factors, and functional limitations.

3. Explanation of Findings & Treatment Plan
Your practitioner will explain their findings and outline a proposed treatment plan tailored to your specific needs and goals.

4. Informed Consent
Before any hands-on care begins, your informed consent will be obtained. You are always in control of your treatment.

5. Post-Treatment Re-assessment
Following treatment, your practitioner will reassess to evaluate the response and provide home-care recommendations to support your recovery between sessions.

Do you bill WorkSafeBC or ICBC?

We do not bill directly to WorkSafeBC or ICBC. We will always provide the patient with a receipt for reimbursement from their adjuster or lawyer. RMTs will still treat WorkSafe and ICBC patients.

Do you bill MSP?

We do not bill MSP (BC Medical Services Plan). Payment is required at the time of your appointment and a receipt will be provided.

What is your cancellation policy?

We require 24 hours' notice for any cancellations or appointment changes, regardless of the circumstances. Late cancellations or missed appointments are subject to a fee. Because no treatment was provided, this fee cannot be billed to your insurance plan and will be charged to the credit card on file.

Do I need to shower before my appointment?

We recommend showering the day of your treatment to help you feel comfortable and to support a clean treatment environment.

Can I come in if I'm feeling sick?

If you have symptoms such as fever, cough, sore throat, or stomach illness, we ask that you reschedule your appointment to protect both patients and staff.

Why do you charge no-show fees?

Our clinic operates by appointment only, and each session is reserved specifically for you. When an appointment is missed or cancelled without sufficient notice, we are unable to offer that time to another patient who may be waiting for care. No-show fees help ensure fairness to both our practitioners and the patients who rely on timely access to treatment.

I need to use up my benefits before year-end, but can't find an available therapist?

Our clinic provides medically necessary treatments, and we do not book appointments solely for the purpose of using remaining benefits. Each session must be clinically appropriate and documented according to regulatory standards.

To help you secure the appointments you need, we encourage booking your sessions in advance. You can also add yourself to our waitlist, or ask us to add you — we actively contact patients as openings become available.

Can other family members use my unused benefits?

No. Extended health benefits cannot be shared or transferred to another person. Benefits are issued to you as the plan member, and using them for someone else is considered insurance fraud. For every appointment, our billing must legally match the patient's name, date of treatment, and treatment duration exactly as documented by the practitioner.
