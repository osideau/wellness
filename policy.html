— Clinic Policy

Our policies,
clearly stated.

These policies exist to protect both patients and practitioners, and to ensure every visit is safe, respectful, and effective.

Accuracy of Information

Patients are responsible for ensuring that all personal, medical, and contact information provided to the clinic is accurate and up to date. Accurate information supports safe, effective, and appropriate care.

Privacy, Confidentiality & Information Sharing

Information Collection
The clinic and its health professionals collect personal and medical information as needed to provide safe, effective treatment and maintain accurate clinical records.

Communication With Other Providers
With patient consent, the clinic may communicate with family physicians or referring practitioners when necessary to support continuity of care.

Confidentiality & Disclosure
All personal and medical information is kept confidential and will only be disclosed with patient authorization or when required by law.

Cancellation Policy

We require 48 hours' notice for cancellations or appointment changes. This allows us to offer the time to another patient and ensures our practitioners' schedules are used effectively.

Late Cancellations
Appointments cancelled with less than 48 hours' notice cannot be cancelled through your Jane account, as the system automatically locks the appointment. In these cases, the credit card on file will be charged according to clinic policy.

Insurance Billing
Insurance cannot be billed for missed or late-cancelled appointments, as no treatment was provided. The full appointment fee is the responsibility of the patient.

Late Arrival Policy

To maintain fairness and minimize disruption, appointments will end at the originally scheduled time even if a patient arrives late. Treatment will be provided for the remaining time available. The full appointment fee applies regardless of the amount of treatment time used.

Direct Billing Policy

Insurance Card Requirements
To ensure accurate and efficient direct billing, patients must provide clear photos of both the front and back of their insurance card. The front confirms identity and policy details; the back provides claim submission information and insurer contact details. Providing both sides helps prevent delays and ensures claims are submitted correctly.

Coverage Details
The clinic does not have access to individual plan details, including remaining coverage. Patients are responsible for contacting their insurance provider directly with any benefit-related questions before their appointment.

Professional Conduct & Boundaries

Treatment Standards
All treatments follow CCHPBC Standards of Practice, including informed consent, clinical reasoning, and safe, evidence-informed care.

Patient Consent
Patients may modify or withdraw consent at any time during treatment. Practitioners will adjust or discontinue treatment accordingly.

Behaviour & Safety
Any inappropriate, sexual, aggressive, or unsafe behaviour will result in immediate termination of the session. The full appointment fee will be charged.

Privacy & Confidentiality of Records

Record Security
All patient information is stored securely in accordance with PIPA and professional standards for privacy and record-keeping.

Access to Records
Patients may request access to their clinical records at any time. The clinic will respond promptly to all record requests.

Disclosure of Records
Records will not be shared with third parties without written patient consent unless required by law.
